### 2. AI names are no longer treated as one homogeneous theme

The new classification separates:

- Platform/hyperscaler.
- Diversified supplier.
- Cyclical supplier.
- Application/data owner.
- Speculative bottleneck beneficiary.

This is especially important for the current model portfolio because MRVL, AMD, WDC, and STX all express AI infrastructure upside, but they do not have the same business resilience or capex-cycle sensitivity.

### 3. Flow fragility becomes a portfolio-level warning

The 2026-06-05 AI/semiconductor selloff exposed a gap in the prior workflow: single-stock stops existed, but there was not yet a portfolio-level crowding/flow warning for synchronized AI-theme weakness.

The new overlay is designed to catch this earlier by watching narrow leadership, semiconductor crowding, options/call demand, volatility behavior, buyback windows, systematic exposure proxies, and breadth.

### 4. Backtest path is now explicit

The next research step is not to promote rules immediately. It is to test:

- Plain pullback buy versus trend-aligned reclaim buy.
- Flow-fragility sizing filter.
- AI quality/capex-cycle score.
- Sleeve-level factor-macro drawdown overlap.
