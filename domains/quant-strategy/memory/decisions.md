## 2026-05-29

- Data source baseline: Tencent Smartbox for fuzzy search, Tencent quotes as primary, Sina quotes as fallback.
- All quote text responses should be decoded as GBK.
- Strategy analysis should consume normalized quote objects rather than raw endpoint payloads.
- Quote outputs should preserve `source` for debugging and data-quality review.
- Runtime caveat: old Node can fetch Tencent data but may not decode GBK Chinese names correctly without a decoder.
- Repository memory model: use layered storage with root-level `docs/`, `domains/`, `skills/`, `templates/`, and `archive/`. Stable conclusions live in `memory/decisions.md`; running notes live in `memory/strategy-log.md`; factual contracts live in `references/`; reusable workflows live in `skills/`.
- Strategy market focus: prioritize US-listed stocks and ETFs first. Treat A/H/other markets as later extensions, not the initial strategy target.
- Initial strategy direction: build a daily, long-only, medium-frequency US equity strategy before adding complex alternative data or intraday execution.
- Portfolio design: use a dual-sleeve structure with 50% value-investing allocation and 50% hot-industry momentum allocation. Each sleeve should have independent selection, exit, and review rules.
- Market panic framework: daily strategy analysis must include a market fear gate before stock-level recommendations. Use VIX level/spike/term structure, SPY/QQQ/SMH drawdown and trend, breadth proxies, and credit-risk proxies to classify `normal`, `elevated`, `stress`, or `panic`; then scale new buy exposure and cash floor accordingly.
- Market fear gate validation: 2026 YTD test supports keeping the gate as a risk governor. It lowered V5 return but improved max drawdown and volatility while maintaining benchmark outperformance. Do not use it as a stock selector; use it for exposure scaling, cash floor, and no-new-buy decisions.
- Portfolio concentration preference: daily recommendations should target 4 to 6 active stocks, hard maximum 8, across 2 to 3 themes. Avoid broad baskets of many similar names; pick the strongest core names and keep overlapping names on watchlist.
- Daily market monitoring requirement: each trading day must review not only recommended stocks, but also broad market regime, sector/theme leadership, best/worst movers, emotion distribution, and whether the strategy missed, overconcentrated, or needs repair.
- Real-time hot news requirement: daily market monitoring must include current news catalysts and map them to macro, sector, theme, and ticker-level implications. News explains and validates price action but does not override technical filters, market fear gate, or stop rules by itself.

## 2026-06-11

- Current portfolio scope: daily execution, post-close audits, portfolio NAV, stop-trigger tables, and risk reviews should focus only on user/broker-confirmed real-account holdings. The retired USD 20,000 model/paper ledger should not be included in current holdings, current NAV, or routine stop tables unless the user explicitly asks for historical replay/backtest analysis.
- Real-account order-price discipline: every new buy limit recommendation must state its technical price basis. Acceptable bases include 5/10/20/50-day moving averages, intraday VWAP, prior low/support, gap level, ATR-derived pullback range, or explicit risk-line offset. A buy price that is only an intuition/discount from the current quote must be labeled as a starter-test price, not a technical support price.
- For the real HKD 20,000 account, single-share sizing makes entry quality more important. New buys should prefer technically justified levels over high-probability fills, and should avoid using margin/financing. If a recommended buy would consume more than roughly 10%-15% of account capital or lacks a clear technical basis, it should default to watch-only.
- Strategy architecture update: add the Double-Radar satellite strategy as an aggressive, high-elasticity module alongside the V5 core. Default research mix is `75%` V5 Optimal plus `25%` Double-Radar Top5 gated. This module is for satellite exposure and theme discovery, not a replacement for V5 or for market-fear gating. See `references/double-radar-satellite-strategy.md`.
- Two-path entry discipline: every actionable buy candidate must evaluate both `pullback entry` and `breakout/confirmation entry`. Pullback entries require technical support basis such as MA/VWAP/support/ATR. Breakout entries require broad-market confirmation, usually QQQ and SMH risk-on behavior, plus stock-level confirmation such as reclaiming a trigger level, holding above opening range/VWAP, or outperforming SMH for 15-30 minutes. Reports must also state a no-chase line and a failure/stop condition.
- For the real HKD 20,000 account, breakout/confirmation entries are allowed only as small starter positions when market confirmation is strong. They should not exceed roughly 5%-10% account exposure per order, should not use margin, and should be cancelled if the trigger fails intraday. This prevents the strategy from being trapped into only waiting for low-probability pullbacks during strong repair/risk-on sessions.
- Six-dimensional trade decision checklist: every real-account buy/sell recommendation must explicitly evaluate market sentiment, theme strength, stock relative strength, technical entry quality, account constraints, and exit/risk plan before producing an action. The final output must map those dimensions into one of `buy`, `conditional buy`, `wait`, `watch only`, `reduce`, or `exit`. If any dimension is uncertain because live data is unreliable, mark the trade as conditional or wait rather than presenting it as executable.

## 2026-06-17

- Daily K-line technical check is mandatory before any real-account buy, add, reduce, or exit recommendation. At minimum, review the latest daily close/open/high/low plus 5/10/20/50-day moving averages, 20-day high/low, and whether the stock is extended above or broken below those levels. Intraday trigger prices such as opening-range breaks, VWAP reclaims, or stop-limit levels are not sufficient by themselves.
- Add/pyramid rule using daily K-line context: do not add to a position solely because it is up intraday. A core add requires either support-based entry near a relevant daily MA/support zone, or a breakout that clears a daily resistance/opening range while the stock is still not excessively extended versus the 5/10/20-day averages. If the stock is far above the 20-day MA, use hold/profit-protection rather than new buying unless the user explicitly accepts high-extension risk.
- Weak-position averaging-down rule: do not average down a stock that is below its 5/10/20-day moving averages unless it has reclaimed a predefined risk line and shows market/theme confirmation. A stock sitting only near the 50-day MA after losing shorter-term averages is a weak rebound candidate, not an automatic add.
- Daily K-line output rule: every actionable order recommendation should include a short technical basis line naming the relevant daily MA/support/resistance or extension state, plus the fee-adjusted breakeven and cancellation/exit rule.
- Strong-trend participation module: do not automatically reject a leading stock only because it is extended above daily moving averages. If the theme is the clear market leader, the stock is a top relative-strength name, market gate is not `stress` or `panic`, and one share fits account risk, the strategy must present a controlled participation plan instead of watch-only. Valid plans are: a small one-share breakout stop-limit above a fresh daily/intraday high, a pullback limit near opening-range/VWAP/support, or an explicit no-trade decision with the reason.
- Missed-rally prevention rule: when a watchlist name is up strongly for multiple sessions or is repeatedly among the theme leaders, the report must label it as `trend participation candidate`, `too extended but actionable on pullback`, or `not actionable due to account/fee/risk`. Do not leave it as generic watch-only without a concrete trigger.
- High-price single-share exception: for the HKD 40,000 account, a USD 600-750 stock can be considered for a one-share trend participation order even if it is about 12%-15% of account value, provided the stop risk is predefined and the expected loss is acceptable. USD 1,000-class stocks remain generally too large unless explicitly approved by the user.

## 2026-06-20

- Notional price limit for small accounts: for accounts with total NAV below USD 10,000, stocks with a share price above USD 300 are prohibited from the satellite sleeve (3%-6% target weight). They may only be held in the core sleeve where a 10%-15% weight is appropriate. Satellite candidates must have a share price below USD 150 so that 1-2 shares represent a true 3%-6% weight and do not distort account risk controls. This rule was derived from the June retrospective analysis where a 1-share RDW position at USD 15 represented a correct satellite weight, but a 1-share MRVL at USD 310 would represent a core-sized position placed in a satellite sleeve.
- Hard cut priority and workflow patch: individual stock stop-loss execution has the highest absolute priority in the system, overriding all macro fear gate states. Once a stock's completed close confirms a breach of its stop line, the position must be reduced or exited within the first 15 minutes of the next session opening. The system must not defer execution by labeling it as a passive `exit/stop-review` item awaiting user confirmation for more than one trading day. If stop execution is not confirmed within one trading day, the next automation report must open with a maximum-severity alert restating the pending stop, not silently carry it forward as a routine review item. This addresses the structural workflow deficiency identified in the June retrospective where RDW was held three days past its stop line due to an `awaiting user broker confirmation` loop.
- Capital sizing price-gate after baseline change: when the real-account capital baseline increases, existing positions whose portfolio weight has been diluted below their target weight are prohibited from being rebuilt by chasing at current market price. Specifically, new buy orders for diluted existing positions are only permitted under one of two technical conditions: (1) a pullback entry where the stock retraces to a key daily moving average or support zone approximately 3%-5% below the current price, placed as a limit order; or (2) a breakout entry where the stock clears a fresh daily or intraday resistance level and the market fear gate confirms a risk-on state, and the entry rationale is the technical breakout itself rather than restoring portfolio weight. There is no fixed waiting period; the constraint is price-based, not time-based. Deploying new capital into entirely new positions with valid technical setups is not restricted by this rule. Directly averaging up into diluted existing positions at current market price purely to restore portfolio weight is prohibited.

## 2026-06-27

- Intraday position-build speed cap: in an `elevated` market fear regime, the total portfolio equity exposure must not increase by more than 8 percentage points across all confirmed real-account fills on any single trading day. In a `normal` fear regime, the single-day cap is 15 percentage points. In `stress` or `panic`, no new buys are permitted. The cap is measured using confirmed fills only, not open orders. This rule was derived from the 2026-06-25 execution review where three correlated AI-capex fills on a single day raised total exposure from approximately 22% to 47%, breaching the staged-deployment principle.
- Same-day same-theme new-position cap: within a single trading day, the total notional value of new buy fills for stocks or ETFs belonging to the same broad theme (for example, all AI Capex names including optical, memory, PCB, and compute) must not exceed 15% of current NAV. This rule stacks on top of the intraday speed cap; the more restrictive of the two governs. This rule was derived from the 2026-06-25 review where DRAM plus MXL plus MU were all added on the same day, all expressing the same AI-capex theme.
- Earnings event cooldown rule: when a stock rises more than 10% on its earnings or major-catalyst day, buying that stock or a closely correlated ETF is prohibited on the event day (T+0) and the following day (T+1). The earliest permitted entry is T+2, provided that the price has stabilized (intraday range less than 3% of price), volume has normalized to below 50% of the event-day volume, and the market fear gate has not worsened. This rule was derived from the 2026-06-25 MU buy at $1155 on the day after the blowout earnings print, which immediately entered a near-stop state when the stock gave back 6.7% the following day.
- Profit-based trailing stop rule: once a core position reaches an unrealized gain of 15% above cost, the formal completed-close stop line must be raised to at least cost-plus-8% (locking in 8% profit). At 25% unrealized gain, the stop line must be raised to at least cost-plus-15%. At 40% or more unrealized gain, the stop line must be raised to at least cost-plus-25%. The trailing stop line may only move upward, never downward. Every formal post-close audit must check whether any position has crossed a profit threshold and update the stop line if so. Satellite positions use a 25% trailing stop from their high-water close, consistent with the Double-Radar satellite rule. This rule was derived from the 2026-06-27 review where GLW reached a 22% unrealized gain but the stop line remained at $210, capturing less profit than the trailing-stop mechanism would have secured.
- Theme concentration hard caps: the combined weight of all positions in a single sub-theme (for example AI HBM memory, combining MU and DRAM) must not exceed 25% of NAV. The combined weight of all positions in a single broad theme (for example the AI Capex chain, including optical, interconnect, PCB, memory, and compute names) must not exceed 55% of NAV. If the broad-theme total reaches 40%, any incremental buy in that theme requires an equal-notional reduction of an existing same-theme position before the buy is permitted. All calculations use confirmed real-account positions only. This rule was derived from the 2026-06-27 review where all five real-account positions were in the same AI-capex chain, offering no cross-theme diversification during the 2026-06-23 semiconductor drawdown.
- Satellite versus core sleeve sizing rule: for the HKD 50,000 real account (approximately USD 6,400), a satellite position targets 3% to 6% of NAV (approximately $195 to $390), which at a $90 share price corresponds to roughly 2 to 4 shares. A core position targets 8% to 15% of NAV (approximately $520 to $975), corresponding to roughly 6 to 10 shares at $90 or 1 share at a $500 to $750 price. If a position's current market value exceeds the upper boundary of its classified sleeve, it must be reclassified at the next formal post-close audit and its stop-loss standard must be updated to match the new classification. Specifically, MXL held at 6 shares at approximately $90.70 equals approximately $544 or 9% of NAV as of 2026-06-27, which exceeds the satellite ceiling and must be reclassified as a core position with the stop line raised from $86 to approximately $91 to $92.
