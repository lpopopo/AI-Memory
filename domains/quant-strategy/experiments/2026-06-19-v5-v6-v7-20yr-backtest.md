## Strategic Conclusions

1. **V5 remains the CAGR King:** V5 Universe Optimal achieved the highest raw returns with a CAGR of **20.41%** and a Sortino of **1.29**. This is primarily driven by its simple and concentrated momentum scoring (`m63 + m126`) and the optimal 30% individual stock trailing stop-loss.
2. **V7 offers superior drawdown management:** The V7 Full Hybrid version, which integrates the Market Fear Gate and the Double-Radar satellite sleeve, achieved the lowest drawdown of **-31.05%** (a 2.09% improvement over V5) while maintaining a highly competitive CAGR of **19.58%** and Sharpe of **0.97**. This suggests V7 is the most robust and behaviorally comfortable strategy for full-cycle real-money execution.
3. **V6 underperforms as a standalone core:** Standalone V6 Multi-Factor (CAGR **15.92%**, Max Drawdown **-35.14%**) underperformed both V5 and V7. Adding short-term reversal penalties and volatility-adjusted momentum metrics without the fear gate scaling or satellite sleeves does not improve results over simple momentum in a long-term backtest.
4. **Massive benchmark outperformance:** All three versions significantly beat SPY and QQQ benchmarks, proving the value of the dual-sleeve stock-ETF allocation model, trend filters, and stock-level trailing stops.
